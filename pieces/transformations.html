Two transformations, told in the charts a CV can't carry.

Field-operations procurement is buried in transactional noise while the real spend — and the real risk — sit concentrated and unmanaged. The job is always the same: stop hand-touching the noise, put expert attention on the concentration. Two cases, both companies anonymized.

Case 01 · oilfield-services operator · ~$100M spend

A procurement function drowning in noise

An oilfield-services operator in Argentina: roughly $100M of annual spend across 47,000+ purchase orders, a team buried in transactional work, no category management, maturity diagnosed near the bottom of the local oil & gas benchmark.

Built category management around the concentrated spend, pushed the low-value tail onto blanket orders and P-cards so it stopped consuming buyers, simplified the approval chains, and stood up e-sourcing and KPIs — resizing the team toward expertise, not transaction count.

Structural savings in the millions per year, a measurably higher-maturity function, and buyers freed from the tail to work the spend that actually moved the company.

Case 02 · drilling-services operator · supply chain build-out

Building a strategic supply chain under siege

A drilling-services operator in Argentina, mid-2010s: import restrictions, currency controls, a government local-content mandate, and a fleet growing five- to six-fold — all at once. The supply chain had to become a strategic function while keeping rigs supplied through the crunch.
