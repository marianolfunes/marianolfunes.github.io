Procurement Operations: Solving the Red Queen effect

Mariano Funes · March 4, 2024

A famous quote from Lewis Carroll's classic: "Through the Looking-Glass": The Red Queen tells Alice "Now, here, you see, it takes all the running you can do, to keep in the same place." Tied to the notary gigs, red tape, complex processes, and lack of digitalization the Red Queen effect is one of the biggest Procurement department pitfalls: Resources time and soul are consumed by worthless transactions and operations. At the end of the day and weeks people works as hell only to be in the same place, just delivering transactions.

The same transaction volume, re-sorted: blanket orders and catalogs absorb the bulk, non-procurable spend is taken out, and manual transactions shrink to a managed target.

Action Plan

Need to address processes in order to free resources and be able to achieve the fun stuff: Category Management. To have optimized Procurement Operations need to focus in transactions and processes: The following steps address both of them:

1.Transactional Analysis
  • Data Capture
  • Data Analysis
  • Procurement Procedures & Authorization Manual
  • ERP process flow and usage review
2.Fixing
  • Transactional-reviewed Procurement Procedures & Authorizations Manual
  • Non-Procurable Spend: Redefine categories
  • Procurable Spend: Threshold for Procurement involvement for non-core categories
  • Procurable Spend: Blanket Orders boost Plan and implementations
  • Manual transactions: Increase time coverage
  • Other: Corporate and Procurement cards use and catalogs
  • ERP leverage: Digitalization and document repositories.
3.Continuous improvement
  • Manual transactions reduction as a target
