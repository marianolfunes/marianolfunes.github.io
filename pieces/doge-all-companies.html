All companies need a DOGE

Incremental cost reductions are not enough
Mariano Funes · May 21, 2025

Led by Elon, Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) is a temporary US Government Organization that focuses on cutting wasteful spending and modernizing government.

DOGE reported significant cost savings introducing Spend Control, Organizational Optimization and Deregulation Efforts to the US Government. Savings so far are estimated to 170 bn to May 11th.

Estimated Savings
$170B
Combination of asset sales, contract/lease cancellations and renegotiations, fraud and improper payment deletion, grant cancellations, interest savings, programmatic changes, regulatory savings, and workforce reductions.

Also it organized in small cells and seems to follow a specific methodology.

the methodology, in five steps
StepNameShort Description
1Identify InefficienciesReview federal spending and operations to pinpoint waste and redundancies.
2Terminate Unnecessary AgreementsCancel non-essential contracts, grants, and leases based on inefficiencies.
3Repeal Burdensome RegulationsEliminate outdated or restrictive regulations to reduce compliance costs.
4Optimize Workforce and TechnologyImplement workforce reductions and modernize processes with technology upgrades.
5Report TransparentlyDocument actions and outcomes publicly for accountability.

The US Government but also all companies were built on decisions taken over the years like geological layers.

Companies relies on a standard rule of X% savings target over years, well aligned to a yearly budget process.

But is this enough today?

With current disruption environment, with hyper-accelerating technology and continuous disruptions, seems not to be the case.

What is differential in the DOGE methodology compared to the standard procurement cost savings and organizational restructuring processes?

doge methodology vs. standard corporate practice
StepNameDOGE MethodologyStandard Corporate PracticeDifferential (DOGE)
1Identify InefficienciesCentralized, data-driven review across all departments.Budget-driven with incremental focus.Centralized analytics
2Terminate Unnecessary AgreementsMass termination of contracts, grants, leases.Prioritizing continuity.Mass termination
3Repeal Burdensome RegulationsRapid repeal using executive authority.Bureaucratic compliance.Regulation repeal
4Optimize Workforce and TechnologyRapid, cross-department workforce and tech optimization.Gradual workforce growth; slow tech adoption.Rapid optimization
5Report TransparentlyPublic, real-time dashboard reporting.Limited internal reporting; periodic disclosures.Real-time transparency

DOGE methodology seems an opportunity especially for companies playing in markets being under disruption.

Any corporation will be brave enough to implement DOGE?
